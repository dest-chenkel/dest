## 1. Johannes Kepler (1571–1630)

- **On thinking God’s thoughts:**  
  “I was merely thinking God’s thoughts after him…”
- **On geometric beauty:**  
  “The geometry of the universe is the most efficacious symbol of the glory of God.”

---

## 2. Isaac Newton (1643–1727)

- “This most beautiful system… could only proceed from the counsel… of an intelligent and powerful Being.”
- “Nature is pleased with simplicity.”
- “The great ocean of truth lay all undiscovered before me.”

---

## 3. James Clerk Maxwell (1831–1879)

- “Teach us to study the works of Thy hands.”
- Maxwell famously used **symmetry** as a guide, adding terms until the equations “looked complete” — revealing that **light is an electromagnetic wave**.

---

## 4. Albert Einstein (1879–1955)

- “I want to know how God created this world… the rest are details.”
- “Everything should be made as simple as possible, but not simpler.”
- “Behind anything that can be experienced there is something… whose beauty and sublimity reaches us only indirectly.”

---

## 5. Werner Heisenberg (1901–1976)

- “The first gulp from the glass of natural sciences will turn you into an atheist, but at the bottom of the glass God is waiting for you.”
- “Beauty is the proper conformity of the parts to one another and to the whole.”

---

## 6. Paul Dirac (1902–1984)

- “God is a mathematician of a very high order…”
- “A theory with mathematical beauty is more likely to be correct…”
- “Nature’s fundamental laws are described in terms of a mathematical theory of great beauty.”

---

## 7. Richard Feynman (1918–1988)

- “The rules of the game are what we mean by fundamental physics.”
- “Nature uses only the longest threads to weave her patterns…”

---

## 8. Steven Weinberg (1933–2021)

- “The beauty of present theories is an anticipation… of the beauty of the final theory.”
- “Convergence of the arrows of explanation…”

---

## 9. Stephen Hawking (1942–2018)

- “We would know the mind of God.”
- “Because there is a law such as gravity, the universe can and will create itself from nothing.”

---

## 10. Frank Wilczek (1951– )

- “Our most fundamental laws… are, to a high degree, beautiful.”
- “The more we understand, the simpler the underlying rules appear…”

---

## 11. Edward Witten (1951– )

- “The beauty of Einstein’s equations… is as real as the beauty of music.”
- “String theory is a miracle through and through…”

---

## 12. Michio Kaku (1947– )

- “When you come up with a theory, you fall in love with the beauty…”
- “To a physicist, beauty means symmetry and simplicity.”
